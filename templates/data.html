CQI Coffee Quality Institute

The data we are using comes from the Coffee Quality Institute, "a non-profit organization working internationally to improve the quality of coffee and the lives of people who produce it". One thing they are known for is promoting a common language about the quality of coffee.

Rating Categories

There are many aspects of coffee that the CQI considers in their rating system:

  • Aroma: the smell of the coffee when infused hot water
  • Flavor: represents the coffee's principal character, the "mid-range" notes
  • Aftertaste: the length of positive flavor qualities remaining after the coffee is swallowed
  • Acidity: is described as "brightness" when favorable and "sour" when unfavorable
  • Body: based on the tactile feeling of the liquid in the mouth
  • Balance: how all the aspects of flavor, aftertaste, acidity, and body work together
  • Uniformity: consistency of flavor of the different cups of the sample tasted
  • Clean Cup: a lack of interfering negative impression from first ingestion to final aftertaste
  • Sweetness: a pleasing fullness of flavor as well as any obvious sweetness
  • Cupper Points: based on the preference of the individual coffee taste-tester
  • Defects: negative or poor flavors that detract from the quality of the coffee

To explore the values of these variables, press the "Change Metric" button next to the histogram above and watch the distribution and mean (denoted by the dotted line) change for each variable.
